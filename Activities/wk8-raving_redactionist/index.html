**TOP SECRET ABSINTHE RECIPE!**

DO NOT DISTRIBUTE

Ingredients

  • Moonshine (80-85%) – 1 liter
  • Bitter wormwood (dried) – 100 gr
  • Anise seeds – 50 gr
  • Fennel seeds – 50 gr
  • Lemon balm – 5 gr (for shade)
  • Hyssop – 10 gr (for shade)

Preparation:

  1. Put wormwood, anise, and fennel into a fermentation vessel—glass bottle or jar. Seal it and leave for 2 weeks in a dark place with room temperature. Shake it once a day.
  2. Pour the obtained infusion along with herbs into a distillation still of classic design (with a heating spiral). Additionally, you can use a thumper. Add 3.5 liters of water to lower infusion’s ABV to 18-20%. Distilling stronger liquid is explosion-hazardous!
  3. Heat up the distillation still on medium heat and then decrease the heat so that the prepared absinthe could drop instead of spouting. The obtained distillate should be cold downstream. Active heating might cause the herbs to burn thus spoiling the taste with bitterness.
  4. Depending on the quality of moonshine the first 30-70 ml of the return yield (2-7% of the initial volume) should be gathered separately. This fraction is called “foreshots”—a poisonous substance with bad smell.
  5. The middle run (or “hearts”) can be gathered until ABV drops to 38-40% or the liquid becomes cloudy. After that, you can finish distillation. During distillation, the distillate changes its color from clear to gold but with no cloudiness. This is normal.
  6. In order to make a green absinthe from moonshine, you need to pour off 50% of the obtained beverage into a separate vessel, add lemon balm and hyssop (you can also add 0.3-0.5 gr of wormwood for extra bitterness), stir, and seal. Leave it for 1-2 days in a dark place. Shake it once a day. Seal the second part of the absinthe.
  7. Filter the colored infusion through cheesecloth, squeeze well, and then mix with the clear part.
  8. Dilute the homemade absinthe with water till you get the desired ABV—65-75%.
  9. Bottle the absinthe for storing and seal it. Leave it in a fridge or cellar for at least 15-20 days before drinking it. This stabilizes and enhances its taste.
